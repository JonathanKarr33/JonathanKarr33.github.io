Graduate Research

As part of the Lucy Institute for Data & Society, I'm conducting research on NLP bias detection regarding homelessness in South Bend. We are comparing online textual biases against people experiencing homelessness (PEH) with data from cities across the United States. The dataset includes small cities such as South Bend as well as larger cities such as San Francisco. We created a lexicon of words related to PEH and are collecting data from social media (Reddit and X), newspapers, and meeting minutes.

Our research proposal paper has been accepted to IJCAI'25 and can be found here. We also have another paper with results that is currently under review. You can also read a university article detailing the project's plan within the Strategic Framework Grant here.

I am advised by Dr. Nitesh Chawla, and the project PIs are Dr. Georgina Curto Rex and Dr. Matthew Hauenstein.

Summer of 2022
