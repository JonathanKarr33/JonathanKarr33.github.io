Conference Overview

Honored to be presenting at the Adaptive Faith conference, exploring how technology intersects with religious practice and discourse in our digital age. This interdisciplinary conference brings together scholars, theologians, and technologists to examine the profound ways technology is reshaping religious experience and understanding.

Organized by the Centre for Inter-Religious Studies at Al-Mahdi Institute, in collaboration with the McLean Centre for the Study of Culture and Values at the Catholic University of America, and supported by the Ansari Institute for Global Engagement with Religion at the Keough School of Global Affairs, University of Notre Dame.

Our research examines how Large Language Models (LLMs) interact with Catholic religious discourse, revealing important insights about the challenges and opportunities technology presents for religious communities.

Research Context

This work emerges from our broader research agenda at the University of Notre Dame's DIAL Lab, where we explore how artificial intelligence intersects with social issues and human values. The intersection of technology and religion represents a critical frontier for understanding how AI systems shape human understanding and practice.

Key Research Questions

  • How do Large Language Models handle religious concepts and terminology?
  • What are the implications of AI-generated religious content for faith communities?
  • How can we develop AI systems that respect religious diversity and authenticity?
  • What role should technology play in religious education and discourse?
